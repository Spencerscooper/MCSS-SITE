Superintendent Zawolo's

2023 New Year message Jan. 1, 2023

To: Employees of the Monrovia Consolidated School System/MCSS Yesterday, December 31, 2022, we bid farewell to 2022; a momentous year for us at the Monrovia Consolidated School System, MCSS, and my inaugural year as Superintendent. I am profoundly thankful to His Excellency Dr. George M. Weah for our preferment and Minister D. Ansu Sonii for his support in the first year of our leadership of the MCSS. Many thanks to Honorable Samuel D. Tweah, Jr and his team at the Ministry of Finance and Development Planning for their tremendous support. We are also grateful to the Director General of the Cabinet, Honorable Jordan Sulunteh, and other cabinet ministers for their unflinching commitment to the education of our children. Our gratitude extends to Senator Prince Moye, the Chairman of the Senate Standing Committee on Education and the Joint Education Secretariat of the House of Representatives and Senate. We remain forever grateful to the House of Representatives and the Liberian Senate for their belief in us.The support of our alumni and former students of the MCSS is also appreciated.

The year was eventful-we achieved so much, and fell short in a couple of our targets, yet we worked together to rebrand the MCSS and delivered quality instruction in a cordial and peaceful atmosphere. For this, I am profoundly grateful to you, our administrators, principals, teachers, security officers, caretakers, parents and guardians, students, and community members. When we came to the position of Superintendent, we were clear about our vision and aspirations. We convened a series of meetings with stakeholders ranging from principals to students. We heard your concerns, cataloged your hope, and involved you in every stage of our initiative to ameliorate the MCSS. Our focus remains on the betterment of the situation at our schools, the improvement of the working condition for all employees, and the plight confronting our commitment to provide quality instruction. We had to make tough decisions, change some policies, reinstitute forgotten structures, restructure some schools, replace some principals, and reassign some teachers. We can boast that throughout the decision-making process, we utilized candid and robust deliberations involving the MCSS Parents Teachers Association (MCSS-PTA), the MCSS Teachers Association (MCSS-TA), the MCSS Principals Association (MCSS-PA), the MCSS Students Union (MCSS-TU), the MCSS Central Office Workers Association, the members of the Senior ManagementTeam (SMT), and a host of other stakeholders. In these deliberations, I learned a lot. I am proud of my leadership style-leading by consensus rather than a top-down approach. I remain committed to this in the coming year. As we commence the New Year, we must reflect on where we were at the dawn of 2022 and where we are now. To these ends, we beg to enumerate our accomplishments consistent with our Five Strategic Initiatives. So, join us as we do it!
■EmployeeWelfare 1. Working collaboratively with the MCSS-TA, we secured the payment of Two Hundred Fifty United States Dollars ($250.00) to each employee in the form of a handshake. 2. We provided and continue to provide a breakdown of gross and net salaries of employees to eliminate uncertainty relative to expected salary. 3. Completed all necessary paperwork and convened several meetings with the relevant authorities to actualize the salary adjustment for eligible employees.The adjustments, we have been informed, will take effect with the passage of the budget for the Fiscal Year 2023. 4. Offered one scholarship to a female secondary school teacher to pursue graduate and possibly post-graduate studies in the People's Republic of China. 5. Concluded negotiations with the United Bank of Africa, UBA, for friendly loans to employees earning a net income of atleastTwo Hundred United States Dollars ($200.00). 6. Secured an agreement for emergency loans to eligible employees through “FinCredit”,a lending institution. 7. Instructed Principals and Vice-Principals of Instruction to begin exploring the initiation of a four-day workweek for instructional personnel to reduce the cost of transportation. 8. Developed plans to purchase a 32-seater bus and commenced the repair of the existing fleet to assist with the transportation of employees 9. Developed plans to launch a $10,000USD Scholarship Fund for employees. 10. Placed on payroll scores of volunteer employees including teachers, security officers, and caretakers. 11. Provided access to education to wards of volunteer employees. 12. Provided end-of-the-year stipend to volunteer security officers and caretakers. ■Academic Excellence 1. The reactivation of the Content Specialist Position to enhance instructional supervision. 2. The conduct of Subject Conferences at the start of Academic 2022/2023. 3. Commenced the re-assignment of teachers to reflect training, experience, qualification, residency, and past performance. 4. The addition of Early Childhood Development, ECD, and Accelerated Literacy Program, ALP, at all elementary schools with space availability. 5. The creation of the position of ECD Coordinator and the running of monthly in-service training for all ECD teachers. 6. Completed the refurbishing of Science laboratories at the D.Twe Memorial High School and reached finishing stage of the refurbishing of Science laboratories at the William V.S.Tubman High School. 7.Developed system wide examination for students in the 5th, 8th and11th grades at all MCSS schools. ■Safe, Secure, Supportive, and Enticing Learning Environment 1. The elimination of fees with the exception of PTA fees which is set at $1,900 Liberian dollars at all MCSS schools. 2. The establishment of free ECD with daily feeding. 3. The roofing and renovation of several schools including the Anna F. Whisnant, C.William Brumskine, Rebecca J.N. Wilson, St.Paul Bridge, Monrovia Elementary Demonstration School, and the Lorma Quarter Elementary School. 4. The construction of a cafeteria at the Pipeline Junior and Senior High School. 5.Fencing or the elevation of fencing at the William V.S.Tubman High School, G.W.Gibson High School, Boatswain Junior and Senior High School, and A.GlennTubman Elementary School. 6.The construction of the first floor of a three-storey annex at the Boatswain Junior & Senior High School. 7.The production and delivery of needed furniture to select schools. 8.The development, with the involvement of administrators, teachers, parents, and students, of a systemwide student handbook. ■Organizationa lEfficiency 1. The development of a plan to restructure the MCSS to include the position of Assistant Superintendent for administration, and the consolidation of departments to reduce the number of directors and thus create an opportunity for salary improvement to attracting highly qualified professionals. 2. The redesigning of the supervisory structure and opening of offices for supervisors to ensure effective monitoring of supervisors and school personnel.

3. The development of a systemwide intranet and continuation of the development of a student information management system. 4. The introduction of students 'identification numbers. 5. Provision of direct funding to schools based on enrollment. 6. Conducted the first-ever Planning Retreat to develop a FiveYear Plan. 7.Facilitated election of the MCSS to a Three-Years Term to the International Examination Committee of the West African Examination Council, WAEC. ■School Community Corporate Partnerships 1. The establishment of a School-Corporate Partnership with Lonestar MTN wihch led to the opening of a computer lab at the G.W.Gibson High School. 2.The engagement with the United States Agency for International Development, USAID which led to a commitment to rekindle the relationship between our two institutions. 3.The securing of a commitment from the Embassy of the People’s Republic of China to consider providing assistance in the future. 4.The painting of the building and delivery of school supplies to the Lorma Quarter Elementary School by CONEX Liberia. 5.The supplying of transparent zinc sheets by the Greater Lioness Club of Monrovia to the Lorma Quarter Elementary School. 6.Establishment of a Partnership with the University of Botswana. 7.Held the first-ever MCSS Homecoming. Yet, as we revel in all of these accomplishments, we are not content-there is much more we can achieve. In fact ,the best is yet to come. As weblook forward to the new year, let me assure you of my continuous commitment to putting employees'welfare front and center of all we do.Only satisfied administrators, teachers, caretakers, and security officers can deliver on the promise to make the MCSS better. I can never ignore you and the need to improve your livelihood. That is why we made it clear in our last meeting with the MCSS-TA that we support a100% gradual increase in salary for all MCSS employees over a five-year period. We have also pledged to recommend to the National Legislature the capping of requests for rental payment from landlords to no more than six months to make housing affordable for teachers and other middle to low-income earners. As we have always indicated, I see myself first as a teacher and then as an administrator. So, what bothers you affects me. But,we cannot be oblivious to the competing priorities faced by the government and must be measured in our demands and aspirations. In this light, though there is no policy being developed by our HR and Administrative Consultant, Mr. Kla Wilson, and in the spirit of togetherness and broad-based decision-making, I am putting on hold, the introduction of our re-worked Employee Handbook pending the review of a committee to be set up by the Senior Management Team. As we prepare to form the committee, I will make the proposed handbook available in electronic form for your review. It is my hope that this decision will in no way be considered a sign of weakness but an indication of my leadership’s commitment to running a confrontation-free MCSS that we all can be proud of. Note that we do not necessarily have to agree on everything but when disagreements arise, we must summon the will to engage and collaborate to forge a common understanding, find an amicable solution and maintain our commitment to making the MCSS better. By this same token, let me caution those who thrive on pandemonium that we will never allow an ill-conceived and selfishly driven agenda to abort the level of tranquility we have laboriously secured at the MCSS.We embolden all to exhibit respect, appreciate the openness practicalized by my administration, and take advantage of our willingness to give each employee a voice. Let’s face the new year with renewed commitment, optimism, and the belief that we are stronger together. Happy NewYear and best wishes for your dreams and aspirations. Hon. IsaacSaye-LakpohZawolo Superintendent/MCSS
